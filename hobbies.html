Some of My Hobbies

Cooking

Since quarantine, I've picked up cooking as a hobby. I still remember back in April, when I was browsing YouTube, I came upon a video by J. Kenji Lopez-Alt, where he made a French omelette in under two minutes! I was then inspired to try making one myself, and during my first attempt, I remember failing and making scrambled eggs instead. Since then, I've improved my cooking skills vastly. Thanks to the help of YouTube, I've been able to learn how to cook so I won't have to rely solely on instant ramen when I go to college! My favorite dishes to cook are pastas(bolognese and pomodoro). I've been able to(decently) cook more dishes such as oyakodon, tonkatsu, chicken katsu, burgers, Japanese curry, omelettes, fried rice, matcha lattes, avocado toast, and much more! It's been a valuable life skill that I've been able to develop and will cherish for the rest of my life.

Here are some YouTubers I watch and look for inspiration in when cooking either for myself or my family:

  • J. Kenji Lopez-Alt
  • Binging With Babish
  • Adam Ragusea

Steak seasoned with salt and pepper, pan-seared and butter basted

Steak taco - flour tortilla, pan-seared steak, avocado, red onions, cheese

Bolognese pasta - ground beef, carrots, onions, tomato sauce, cream, parmigiano reggiano

Running

Also since quarantine, partially as a result of the hobby above and the staleness of everything being online, I've also been gaining some weight. To counteract this, I've gotten into exercising on the treadmill! As of right now, I try to run 2 miles, first half a mile at 3mph, and the rest at 5mph, with water breaks every half mile. I typically try to run every 1-2 days, or when my schedule permits, and it's something I'm hoping to continue throughout my life to stay fit.

The Stock Market

Also since a few months ago(January 2021), I've been interested in the stock market as well, mostly because of what's been happening with GameStop. In my free time, it's been very fun and worthwhile to learn about financial terms and processes, investing, and the stock market in general!
